Social Media Responsibility Checklist

|**Do's**|**Don'ts**|
|-|-|
|Audit your privacy settings regularly to control who can see your personal posts and tagged photos.|Don't post confidential information, such as your student ID number, home address, or private exam details.|
|Think before you post—ask yourself if you would be comfortable with a future employer or professor reading it.|Don't engage with "trolls" or participate in heated online arguments that could escalate into cyberbullying.|
|Use professional platforms like LinkedIn to showcase your projects, certifications, and academic milestones.|Don't post photos involving illegal activities or content that violates university code-of-conduct policies.|
|Give credit where it's due by tagging original creators or citing sources when sharing articles and media.|Don't vent about professors or peers publicly; these posts often find their way back to the individuals involved.|
|Verify information before sharing news or "viral" updates to avoid spreading misinformation.|Don't ignore your digital footprint—avoid the "delete solves everything" mindset, as screenshots are permanent.|
