**The Digital Safety Checklist**



**Financial \& UPI Safety (Crucial)**

The "PIN for Payments Only" Rule: Never enter your UPI PIN to receive money. Scammers often send "Collect Requests" or QR codes claiming you’ve won a prize; if you enter your PIN, money will be deducted from your account, not added.

Verify the Name After Scanning: When scanning a QR code at a local tapri or shop, always wait for the name to appear on your screen and verify it with the merchant before hitting 'Pay.' Fraudsters sometimes paste fake QR stickers over legitimate ones.



**Account \& Password Security**

Use "Passphrases," Not Passwords: Instead of Karan@123, use a long sentence like ILoveEatingSamosasInTheRain!. It’s much harder for AI-driven "brute-force" attacks to crack but easier for you to remember.



Enable App-Level Locks: Don't just rely on your phone's main screen lock. Use the built-in "App Lock" features for WhatsApp, Instagram, and your banking apps (Biometric/FaceID) to prevent unauthorized access if your phone is borrowed or stolen



**Connectivity \& Browsing**



Avoid Public Wi-Fi for Banking: College or café Wi-Fi is great for research but terrible for transactions. Hackers can "sniff" data on open networks. Use your mobile data (5G/4G) or a trusted VPN when accessing your student portal or bank account.

Spot the "Urgency" Trap: If you get an email or SMS saying your "Library access is blocked" or "SIM card KYC expired" with a link to "Fix it now," it’s almost certainly a phishing scam. Always go directly to the official website instead of clicking the link.



**Device \& Social Safety**

Sideloading is a No-Go: Never download "Modded" versions of apps (like "WhatsApp Gold" or premium versions of Spotify/Netflix) from third-party websites. These APKs often contain "keyloggers" that record every single character you type—including your passwords.



The "Second Channel" Verification: If a friend or family member suddenly messages you on Telegram or WhatsApp asking for an urgent money transfer, call them on a normal phone call first. Scammers use AI voice/video clones (Deepfakes) to impersonate loved ones.





**How to Report Cyber Crime**

If you suspect you've been scammed or see something suspicious, acting fast is key—especially for financial fraud where the first two hours (the "Golden Hour") are critical for freezing funds.
