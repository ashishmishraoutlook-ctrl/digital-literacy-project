**Case Study: The "Urgent Invoice" Phishing Attack**

Phishing is a type of social engineering cybercrime where attackers deceive individuals into revealing sensitive information—such as login credentials, credit card numbers, or corporate data—by masquerading as a trustworthy entity in electronic communications.



**How it Happens: A Step-by-Step Scenario**

In a typical "Business Email Compromise" (BEC) scenario, the attack follows a calculated sequence:



**Lure Generation:** The attacker creates a spoofed email that appears to come from a known vendor or a high-level executive (CFO). The email contains a sense of urgency, often claiming an "overdue payment" or a "security breach."



**The Bait:** The victim receives the email containing a malicious link or an attachment labeled "Invoice\_9921.html."



**The Hook:** Clicking the link redirects the victim to a fraudulent website that perfectly mimics a legitimate login portal (e.g., Microsoft 365).



**Data Harvest:** Unsuspecting, the victim enters their credentials. The attacker captures these in real-time.



**Exploitation:** The attacker uses the stolen credentials to access the corporate network, divert funds, or steal intellectual property.



**Targets and Consequences**

While anyone with an email address is a potential target, attackers often focus on finance departments, HR personnel, and elderly individuals due to their access to funds or perceived technical vulnerabilities.



The consequences are often devastating. For individuals, it leads to identity theft and financial loss. For organizations, a single successful phish can result in massive data breaches, regulatory fines, and irreparable reputational damage. According to the FBI’s 2023 Internet Crime Report, phishing remains the most prevalent threat, accounting for billions in global losses annually.
